## CESR's Positron Source
**File:** `1505.01406v1.pdf`

**Author:** A. Mikhailichenko (Wilson Laboratory, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY 14853)

Describes a new positron converter and power supply installed at the CESR complex that uses a bi-layer solenoid as a short focusing lens in a Quarter Wave Transformation geometry to collect positrons produced on a tungsten target. The new converter unit doubled the rate of positron accumulation at CESR and is proposed as a model for future positron collection optics for linear colliders.

## Hall Effect Magnetic Regulation Systems for the CESR Injector
**File:** `PAC1989_0357.PDF`

**Author:** C. R. Dunnam (Laboratory of Nuclear Studies, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY 14853)

Presents two distinct Hall effect magnetic regulation systems designed to reduce hysteresis effects in the linac and transfer line magnets used for injection into the CESR synchrotron: a high-precision system for dipole analyzer magnets achieving field stability better than 5 parts in 10⁴, and an inexpensive monolithic silicon Hall sensor system for quadrupole magnets with 0.5% repeatability. Radiation and mechanical stress sensitivity of the InAs Hall sensors were evaluated for long-term viability in the CESR injector environment.

## Accelerator Design for the Cornell High Energy Synchrotron Source Upgrade
**File:** `PhysRevAccelBeams.22.021602.pdf`

**Authors:** J. Shanks, J. Barley, S. Barrett, M. Billing, G. Codner, Y. Li, X. Liu, A. Lyndaker, D. Rice, N. Rider, D. L. Rubin, A. Temnykh, and S. T. Wang (CLASSE, Cornell University, Ithaca, New York 14853, USA)

Describes the design and implementation of the CHESS-U accelerator upgrade, which converts CESR from a two-beam electron-positron collider to a dedicated single-beam synchrotron light source by replacing one-sixth of the storage ring with six double-bend achromats using combined-function dipole-quadrupoles. The new lattice achieves a factor-of-four reduction in global natural emittance while increasing high-energy x-ray flux and providing independent insertion device straights for most CHESS end stations.

## Demonstration of Low Emittance in the Cornell Energy Recovery Linac Injector Prototype
**File:** `PhysRevSTAB.16.073401.pdf`

**Authors:** Colwyn Gulliford, Adam Bartnik, Ivan Bazarov, Luca Cultrera, John Dobbins, Bruce Dunham, Francisco Gonzalez, Siddharth Karkare, Hyeri Lee, Heng Li, Yulin Li, Xianghong Liu, Jared Maxson, Christian Nguyen, Karl Smolenski, and Zhi Zhao (Cornell Laboratory for Accelerator-Based Sciences and Education (CLASSE), Cornell University, Ithaca, New York 14853, USA)

Presents a detailed measurement and simulation of the six-dimensional phase space of the electron beam from the Cornell ERL Photoinjector, a high-brightness, 1.3 GHz DC photoemission source, achieving normalized transverse emittances of 0.23 ± 0.02 μm (horizontal) and 0.14 ± 0.01 μm (vertical) at 19 pC/bunch with excellent simulation-measurement agreement. These results demonstrate beam quality at least 20 times better than existing storage rings at equivalent energy spread, representing a milestone for high-brightness, high-current photoinjectors.

## A Novel Compact and Portable Linear Accelerator Electron Beam System
**File:** `A_Novel_Compact_and_Portable_Linear_Accelerator_Electron_Beam_System.pdf`

**Authors:** R. Bruce Miller, Henry Trujillo, Richard W. Johnson, Andrey D. Andreev, Don Smith, Alberto Lopez, Nathaniel P. Lockwood, Daniel A. Enderich, John R. Harris, Samuel C. Schaub, and Michael R. Lambrecht (IEEE Transactions on Plasma Science, Vol. 51, No. 4, April 2023)

Describes the design and demonstration of a lightweight (~250 kg), portable 15 MeV electron beam LINAC system (SPEBS) for research applications. Uses a commercial off-the-shelf E2V MG6028 magnetron to drive an S-band biperiodic π/2 standing-wave accelerator with 17 full-length cells, achieving ~15 MeV with ampere-class peak current at up to 3500 Hz repetition rate. The system fits within ~2 m³ and can be broken into two person-portable 50 kg components.

## Electronic Irradiation of Foods: Principles of Electron Acceleration in Microwave Accelerators
**File:** `0-387-28386-2.pdf`

**Author:** R. B. Miller (EBM, LLC, Albuquerque, New Mexico; Springer, 2005, Chapter 6, pp. 137–172)

Chapter 6 of the book *Electronic Irradiation of Foods: An Introduction to the Technology*. Covers the fundamental physics of electron acceleration in microwave structures, including electron motion in DC and oscillating fields, beam formation in electron guns, resonant electromagnetic cavity theory, coupled-cavity linac structures, and methods for coupling power from a microwave source into an accelerating structure. Uses numerical simulations to illustrate how confined microwave fields accelerate electrons.

## Modelling SPEBS + Spectrometer Design
**File:** `Spebs Modelling and Spectrometer.pdf`

**Authors:** Jared Maxson and Matt Andorf (Cornell CLASSE + Physics, June 2, 2026)

Presentation slides describing the effort to model SPEBS (the ~15 MeV, ampere peak current, 19 S-band cell portable LINAC from Miller et al.) and design a spectrometer to measure its energy distribution and emittance. Models the RF cavity using a homebrew eigensolver and the DC gun via analytic approximation of a Pierce diode geometry, generating on-axis fieldmaps Ez(z) followed by off-axis Taylor expansion. Highlights the technical challenge of the very high energy spread case for which standard simulation tools are inadequate.

## Injection Optimization of CESR at 3.76 GeV
**File:** `vrdoljak.pdf`

**Author:** Denis Vrdoljak (Santa Clara University, September 3, 2003)

Uses envelope simulations in Fortran, PAW, Perl, and C-Shell to optimize injection parameters for CESR following the installation of six wiggler magnets, studying three lattice configurations: the old 1.88 GeV lattice, the 5 GeV CHESS lattice with wigglers off, and the 1.9 GeV lattice with wigglers on. The old lattice yields efficient injection (~50%) at horizontal tunes of 10.535 and 10.565, while the new wiggler lattices present significant injection challenges requiring further investigation.
