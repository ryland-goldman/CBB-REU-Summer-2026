## 1.7 Equations of motion

The relativistic equations of motion of the individual macro-particles are solved by GPT. The position **x** and the momentum p = 𝛾𝑚v** **are used as the coordinates of a particle. The equations of motion for particle *i* are given by: 𝑑p𝑖 𝑑𝑡= F𝑖 𝑑x𝑖 𝑑𝑡= v𝑖= p𝑖𝑐 √p𝑖 2 + 𝑚𝑖 2𝑐2 [1. 21] The force on particle *i* is calculated using: F𝑖= 𝑞(E𝑖+ v𝑖× B𝑖) [1. 22]

These equations of motion can not be solved for each particle individually because, due to the spacecharge, the force on each particle depends on the position of all other particles. Therefore we introduce the vector **y**(*t*) containing the six coordinates of all the particles as a function of time *t*. The equations of motion [1.21] can then be rewritten as: 𝑑y(𝑡) 𝑑𝑡 = f(𝑡,y(𝑡)) [1. 23] where **f**(*t*,**y**(*t*)) is a combination of the equations [1.21] and [1.22] for every particle. The only boundary conditions are the initial particle coordinates at a specified time.

When additional differential equations are added, the variables are added to **y**(*t*) and the differential equations are added to **f**. The extra differential equations can be a function of all the particle coordinates, time and the variables of other differential equations. The boundary condition, the initial value of the variables, must be supplied for each value individually. Because GPT solves particles coordinates and extra variables simultaneously, the results are always self-consistent. The method used to solve [1.23] is described in the following section.
