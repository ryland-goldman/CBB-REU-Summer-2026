### 3.12.6 Robust estimates: Median and Range

Data analysis based on averages, standard deviations or rms emittances is very sensitive to outliers. This is typically of little concern when analyzing GPT results obtained with a mesh-based space-charge model, but it can be disastrous with point-to-point Coulomb interactions. The reason for this is that these point-to-point interactions cause non-Gaussian heating, leading to a Holtsmark distribution in phase-space. This distribution has incredibly long tails with divergent rms values. Consequently, calculating the average value of a limited number of sample particles gives an extremely inaccurate estimate for the center of the distribution. Using the standard deviation as a measure for the width of the distribution doesnвАЩt work either, and even increasing the amount of particles to be tracked in the hope to improve things doesnвАЩt help. Clearly it is *not* wise to calculate averages, or any statistic that is sensitive to outliers, when outliers are expected to be present. In such cases, use robust estimates such as the median instead of the average, and for example a 50% range instead of the standard deviation. Just as with most actual experiments, these quantities essentially ignore a few outliers, regardless of where these outliers happen to be.
